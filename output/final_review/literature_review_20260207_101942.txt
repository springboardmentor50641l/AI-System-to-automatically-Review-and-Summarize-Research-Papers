The Inception-v3 architecture has been reported to achieve a new state of the art for single crop evaluation on the ILSVRC 2012 classification challenge (20260207_091522_789269). This architecture demonstrated 21.2% Top-1 and 5.6% Top-5 error rates for single frame evaluation, achieved with a computational cost of 5 billion multiply-adds per inference and utilizing less than 25 million parameters (20260207_091522_789269).

Further performance enhancements for Inception-v3 models were observed with multi-crop evaluation and ensemble methods (20260207_091522_789269). A single Inception-v3 model, employing 144-crop evaluation, achieved 18.77% Top-1 and 4.2% Top-5 error (20260207_091522_789269). When an ensemble of four Inception-v3 models was utilized with multi-crop evaluation, error rates were further reduced to 17.2-17.3% Top-1 and 3.5-3.58% Top-5 (20260207_091522_789269). It is noted that these results, with the exception of one top-5 ensemble result, were reported on the validation set (20260207_091522_789269). Beyond performance metrics, the development of Inception-v3 involved the description of general principles and optimization ideas for efficiently scaling convolutional networks (20260207_091522_789269). The architecture demonstrated that high-quality results could be achieved with lower-resolution networks, closely matching higher-resolution counterparts when computational effort was kept constant (20260207_091522_789269). Comparatively, the Inception-v3 solution reportedly uses significantly less computation than other high-performing denser networks, outperforming He et al. [6] while being six times cheaper computationally and using at least five times fewer parameters (20260207_091522_789269). The combination of a lower parameter count with additional regularization techniques, such as batch-normalized auxiliary classifiers and label-smoothing, facilitated the training of high-quality networks on relatively modest-sized training sets (20260207_091522_789269).

The paper aims to serve as a technical reference for academia, industry professionals, and decision-makers in various practical scenarios and application areas (20260207_091528_259631).
